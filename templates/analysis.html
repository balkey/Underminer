    2. Part of speech tagging



    Part of speech tagging is an interesting breed: mostly all longer texts split up into a quite constant array of nouns / verbs / etc. - no surprise here!

    What's more interesting when you combine part of speech tagging with other forms of analysis. Would the occurences of only adjectives tell us more about the mood of a certain part of text, like a chapter? Certainly so! What about verbs? Do they present traces of action and happening?

    Part of speech tagging becomes especially helpful when playing with n-grams and sentiment analysis, so for now just take our word: the application is ready to bring 100.300 English words for tagging, there can not be a lot more than that!

    As you can see, Underminer was able to tag 96.8 percent of all disctinct words in Alice's Adventures in Wonderland - we keep working on that remaining 3.2 percent!

    3. Panel and sentiment analysis



    N-grams and sentiment analysis need a lot of computing and Underminer is no powerhouse. Still, looking for texts around the internet that represent the essence of the basic 8 human emotions (hopefully no more than two at a time) sounds like a fun project, so we are after you haters and believers!

    Until we meet, Underminer has a lot of current shortcomings: it does not understand different part of speech that have the same form (ie. "We sure mine data!" or "This is mine!" and "That is a goldmine!"), it only makes vague guesses at positive and negative sentiments (see the chart below!), and the list is long.

    We will keep working on making Underminer better and more accurate - for the time being, any helping advice is appreciated!
